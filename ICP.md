# Caye — Ideal Customer Profile

The product is no longer "omnichannel inbox." It's a **front-desk AI receptionist that handles inbound and books customers in the owner's voice, with a back-office mode for crew later.** That shift narrows who you should chase. This ICP is the front-desk wedge — the fastest path to a paid $79/mo customer.

## The one-line ICP
**A Caribbean, owner-operated small business that is losing money to missed messages, whose customer questions can be answered from a stable list of services, prices, and hours — and whose owner (or the person who handles comms) is personally drowning in their DMs.**

## What the 3 pilots taught (this ICP is evidence-based, not a guess)
- **Bimini = the fit.** Tour operator, stable tours + prices, husband-wife shop, Karenda (comms handler, not the titular owner) is buried in booking emails around a day job, lives in email, wants delegation ("Caye handles everything"). Adopted through trust in the founder. → **This is the template.**
- **Simply Dave = the cautionary tale.** Same vertical, right pain on paper — but he couldn't access his own Facebook/Meta accounts (3 failed resets) and went silent. **Vertical fit doesn't matter if the owner can't onboard or isn't engaged.** Access + engagement are hard filters.
- **ODS = the wrong wedge (for now).** Construction inbound is *status questions about live jobs* ("when's my house done?"), which Caye can't answer without a job-data layer. Project/state-based businesses are a **back-office expansion play with a long cycle — not the front-desk wedge.**

## Fits (target these)
Businesses whose inbound is answerable from a **stable catalog** (services + prices + hours + availability):
- Tour / excursion operators
- Restaurants & cafés (reservations, hours, menu, catering inquiries)
- Salons, spas, barbershops, nail techs (appointments)
- Small hotels, guesthouses, Airbnb / villa hosts (availability, rates)
- Clinics, dentists, vets (appointment requests)
- Rentals — car, scooter, jet ski, equipment

## Firmographics
- **Geography:** Caribbean-rooted. **Bahamas first** (founder's warm network + pilot proof). Never US-mainland outreach (anti-goal).
- **Size:** owner-operated or husband-wife; 0–10 staff. No internal tech team.
- **Channels:** inbound scattered across WhatsApp / IG / Messenger / email; the owner is the bottleneck.
- **Offering:** stable enough that a price/service list rarely changes week to week.
- **Pain, already happening:** missing or slow to answer messages; has lost at least one booking to it.

## Buyer vs. user
The buyer is **whoever feels the pain of answering messages** — often NOT the titular owner. At Bimini it's Karenda (the owner's wife), not Max. Sell to the person glued to the phone, not the person whose name is on the business.

## How to spot one in the wild
- "DM to book" in the bio, with no real booking system behind it
- Active social presence but visibly slow/inconsistent replies
- Solo owner who's obviously on the tools (on the boat, on the chair, in the kitchen)
- Inquiries pile up overnight / on their day off

## Qualifying questions (ask before pitching)
1. **Who answers your messages today, and how fast?** (finds the bottleneck + buyer)
2. **Have you ever lost a booking because a message got missed?** (surfaces the pain in their words)
3. **Do your services and prices stay mostly the same week to week?** (catalog-stability test — separates front-desk fits from ODS-shape)
4. **Can you log into your own Facebook / WhatsApp Business right now?** (the Dave filter — access friction kills onboarding)

If #3 is "no, every job is custom/quoted" → it's a back-office/longer-cycle prospect, not the wedge. If #4 is "no / I don't know" → onboarding will stall unless you can run BSP Embedded Signup for them.

## Anti-ICP (do not chase)
- **US-mainland** anything (anti-goal — accept inbound, don't outreach).
- **Project/status-based businesses** (construction, contractors, custom fabrication) as a *front-desk* sale — they need the back-office/ops layer first.
- **Owners not personally drowning in comms** — no pain, no urgency, no $79.
- **Price-shoppers** who want the cheapest tool, or anyone who wants to *own the code* (non-negotiable).
- **Anyone you can't reach through trust** — Caribbean SMBs buy from a known local, not a cold pitch deck (decisions-log 2026-05-10). Cold works best warmed by a referral.

## Why $79 fits this ICP
One saved booking (a tour, a table for 8, a weekend villa night) is $100–500+. At $79/mo, Caye pays for itself on ~1 saved booking every 1–2 months. The math only lands for businesses where a *single missed message = real money* — which is exactly this ICP.
